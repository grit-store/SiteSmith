# Local Service Playbook

For contractors, roofers, realtors, plumbers, lawyers, dentists — any business serving a geographic area.

## The economics to keep in mind

Most leads for these businesses come from the Google map pack, not organic blue links. The website's job is (a) to be the authority signal that supports map-pack ranking, (b) to convert the click, and (c) to win long-tail organic terms the map pack doesn't serve. Optimize with that hierarchy in mind — a beautiful #4 organic ranking matters less than the site reinforcing the Google Business Profile.

Proximity is a ranking factor nothing on the website changes. A business pinned in a suburb will structurally struggle for downtown head terms. Set architecture accordingly: own the near geography, then expand.

## Page architecture

**Homepage** — brand + primary service + primary area. Targets "[service] [primary city]". Must state NAP visibly (footer at minimum, matching GBP exactly).

**Service pages** — one per real service. Targets "[specific service] [area]". Real depth: process, timeline, what affects cost (ranges are fine if the profile supports them; no invented prices), FAQs pulled from what customers actually ask.

**Location pages** — ONLY for evidenced areas (see main skill rule). A real location page contains: work actually done there, area-specific detail (housing stock, weather/storm history, permit quirks), photos from local jobs, driving/service logistics. If the page could have any city name swapped in without changing, it is a doorway page — don't ship it.

**Trust page(s)** — about/credentials. Every item sourced from the Claims section.

**Question content** — pages answering high-intent questions: "does insurance cover X", "how long does Y take", "cost of Z in [state]". These win featured snippets and capture people pre-decision. The 90-day roadmap should be mostly these.

## Keyword patterns worth hypothesizing

- Emergency intent: "emergency [service] [city]", "[problem] repair near me" — highest urgency, highest value
- Insurance intent (roofing, restoration): "storm damage [service]", "[service] insurance claim [state]"
- Research intent: "how much does [service] cost", "signs you need [service]"
- Comparison intent: "[material A] vs [material B]" — good roadmap content

Suburb-level terms beat metro head terms for a new site: lower competition, higher proximity match, buyer closer to hiring.

## Schema

Use the most specific type available: `RoofingContractor`, `RealEstateAgent`, `Plumber`, `Attorney`, `Dentist` — fall back to `LocalBusiness` only if nothing fits. Include: name, address, phone (NAP exactly as GBP), geo, areaServed (evidenced areas only), openingHours, url, image. Add `FAQPage` on question content. NO aggregateRating unless the site genuinely displays real reviews with a real count.

## NAP consistency

Name, address, phone must be character-identical across: site footer, contact page, schema, and (user's side) Google Business Profile and citations. Flag any mismatch found during audit as High priority — inconsistent NAP actively suppresses local rankings.

## Report items for the user's half (always include)

- Google Business Profile: correct primary category, all services listed, photos of real jobs monthly, posts
- Reviews: steady velocity beats bursts; reply to all
- Citations: Yelp, BBB, Angi, Nextdoor, chamber — NAP identical everywhere
- Links: suppliers, sponsorships, local press — a few real ones beat directories
