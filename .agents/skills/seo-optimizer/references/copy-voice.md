# Copy & Voice Rules

Read this before writing or rewriting ANY body copy. The point of "humanized": a reader should not be able to tell the copy was machine-written, and it should not read like every other site in the vertical.

## The two hard laws

1. **Claims law**: only facts from the profile's Claims section. No invented years, licenses, guarantees, response times, awards, or "family-owned." A missing fact is a note in the change log, not a blank to fill creatively.
2. **Specificity law**: if a sentence could sit on any competitor's site unchanged, cut or sharpen it. "We handle hail damage claims with Georgia insurers" survives; "we deliver quality you can trust" does not.

## Banned outright

- "Nestled in the heart of", "look no further", "we've got you covered", "your one-stop shop", "unparalleled", "seamless", "elevate", "unlock", "delve", "in today's fast-paced world", "we pride ourselves on"
- Tripled adjectives ("fast, reliable, and affordable")
- Rhetorical-question openers ("Looking for a roofer you can trust?")
- Em-dash chains and "it's not just X — it's Y" constructions
- Any phrase on the client's banned list in the profile

## Required habits

- Contractions. "We'll" not "we will."
- Short sentences mixed with medium ones. Read it aloud in your head; if it drones, vary it.
- Concrete nouns and numbers where the profile provides them (crew size, years, counties, response window)
- Customer's words for the problem, not industry jargon: "leak" before "membrane failure", "storm damage" before "wind uplift event"
- Second person doing something real: "Send us photos of the damage" beats "Contact us today for a free consultation"
- One idea per paragraph; front-load the useful fact

## Keyword handling

Write for the intent, then check the term appears naturally in: title tag, H1, first ~100 words, and once or twice in body. If it doesn't fit naturally, the page targets the wrong term — flag it, don't stuff it. Never repeat a keyword in consecutive sentences. Synonyms and the customer's phrasing count in Google's eyes; use them.

## Length

As long as useful, no longer. A service page that fully answers process, cost factors, and timeline might be 500–800 words. Padding to hit a word count is self-defeating — thin ideas stretched long read as filler to both Google and humans. FAQ answers: 2–4 sentences, direct, answer in the first sentence.

## Voice matching

Before writing, re-read the profile's Voice section and 2–3 existing pages the client wrote themselves (if any). Match register. A roofing crew in Georgia does not sound like a SaaS landing page; a cultural nonprofit does not sound like a contractor. When the client's own voice and these rules conflict on style (not on the two laws), the client's voice wins — note the tension in the change log.

## Self-check before shipping any page

- Could a competitor paste this unchanged? → rewrite
- Does any claim lack a profile source? → remove
- Does it answer the search intent in the first screen? → restructure if not
- Would the client's owner say these sentences out loud? → adjust register
- Grep the page for every banned phrase → zero hits
